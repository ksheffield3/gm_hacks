Safety Driving Guidelines for Teens


Reduce or eliminate distractions:

This is perhaps the most difficult problem for young drivers. Passengers, cell phones, GPS, radio, and just plain daydreaming can take your focus off the road when moments count.

Obey the speed limit:

As a young driver, it's normal to feel excited about driving -- you may even feel like you own the road! But you don't. Stay at or below posted speeds, as speeding is a leading contributor of fatal teen accidents.

Be especially aware at school:

Other students are new drivers, just like you. Inexperienced drivers can be dangerous, so be extra careful when driving in school parking lots and on streets near campus.

Never drive under the influence:

Avoid drunk or impaired driving, and don't accept rides from other drivers who may be impaired. It's always worth it to designate a driver, call a cab, or call your family for help -- it could save your life.

Invest in a safe car:

Buy the safest car you can afford. Ideally, your vehicle will have advanced safety features like anti lock brakes, air bags, and electronic stability control.

Be aware:

Pay attention to traffic, weather, and road conditions. You never know what's up ahead!
